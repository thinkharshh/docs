---
title: Present File
description: "Let the AI show a slide, PDF, image, or video to the participant mid-meeting."
---

A meeting where the AI can talk *and* show beats one where it can only talk. **Present File** gives your AI persona a stack of artifacts it can pull up on screen — a deck during a pitch coaching session, a contract clause during a negotiation roleplay, an X-ray during a clinical scenario, a product demo video during onboarding.

The AI decides when to show what based on the conversation, using a `present_content` tool. The file appears in the meeting as a video-overlay surface that both the participant and the AI can reference.

## Setting it up

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your scenario">
    Switch to the **Settings** tab in the scenario builder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Present Files section">
    This is a separate section from Knowledge Base — same Settings tab, different list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload your files">
    Drag-and-drop or pick from your computer. Each file gets a name and a short description — the AI uses the description to decide when to show that file.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Steer when to present (optional)">
    In your scenario's prompt, mention the file name or describe a trigger ("when they ask about pricing, show the pricing sheet"). The AI follows the cue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported formats and limits

| Limit | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Max per file | 200 MB |
| Max files per scenario | 5 |
| Documents | PDF, PowerPoint (`.pptx`, `.ppt`) |
| Images | JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP, SVG |
| Video | MP4, WebM, MOV, M4V |

Files marked as presentable are also indexed for retrieval — they double as Knowledge Base entries. If you want a file searchable but never shown on screen, attach it to **Knowledge Base** instead.

## How the AI decides

The AI sees each file's name and description. During the meeting, when the conversation reaches a point that matches a file's purpose, it calls the `present_content` tool with that file's ID. The file appears on the meeting screen as a video overlay; the AI then narrates over it or asks the participant to react to it.

You can guide this in your scenario's prompt:

> *In your prompt:* "After the participant introduces themselves, show them the **company-overview.pdf** before asking your discovery questions."

Without explicit prompt instructions, the AI presents files opportunistically based on conversation drift.
