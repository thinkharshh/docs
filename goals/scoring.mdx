After each session ends, the AI evaluator reviews the full transcript and scores the participant against each goal.

## Scoring Process

1. The session transcript is passed to the AI evaluator
2. For each goal, the evaluator applies the scoring instructions
3. It produces a numerical score (percentage) and written feedback
4. An overall average score is calculated across all goals

## Score Ranges

| Range | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 90-100% | Excellent | Exceeded expectations on all criteria |
| 80-89% | Good | Met most criteria with minor gaps |
| 70-79% | Average | Partially met criteria, room for improvement |
| 60-69% | Fair | Significant gaps in performance |
| Below 60% | Poor | Did not meet minimum criteria |

## What Gets Evaluated

The AI evaluator has access to:
- The complete session transcript (everything said by both parties)
- The goal's name, description, and scoring instructions
- The scenario context (persona, instructions)

It does **not** have access to:
- Video/visual information
- Tone of voice or pacing (text-based evaluation only)
- Previous sessions by the same participant

## Improving Score Accuracy

<Tip>
If scores don't match your expectations, the issue is almost always in the scoring instructions. Make them more specific.
</Tip>

- **Add concrete examples** of what high/low performance looks like
- **Define score ranges explicitly** (what earns a 90 vs. a 70?)
- **Use observable behaviors** rather than subjective qualities
- **Test with Playground** and verify scores make sense
