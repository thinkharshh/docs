# Privacy

Waterr Meet is designed with privacy as a core principle. Here's how we handle participant data.

## What data is collected

During a session, the following data may be collected:

| Data | Collected when | Purpose |
|------|---------------|---------|
| Audio | Always (required for conversation) | Real-time AI processing, transcript generation |
| Video | Only if participant enables camera | Vision-based AI capabilities |
| Screen content | Only if participant shares screen | Screen sharing intelligence |
| Name / Email | If provided during join flow | Session identification |
| Transcript | Generated from audio | Review, evaluation, analytics |
| Recording | If recording is enabled | Playback, review |
| Goal scores | If goals are configured | Participant evaluation |

## What data is NOT collected

- Browser history or cookies
- Device information beyond what's needed for the session
- Location data
- Data from other tabs or applications
- Participant data outside of the active session

## Participant controls

Participants have control over their data during and after sessions:

- **Camera** -- Can be turned off at any time
- **Microphone** -- Can be muted at any time
- **Screen sharing** -- Can stop sharing at any time
- **Consent** -- Can decline before the session starts (when enabled)
- **Leave** -- Can end the session at any time

## Creator controls

As a scenario creator, you control:

- Whether sessions are recorded
- Whether consent prompts are shown
- Which capabilities are enabled (camera, screen share, etc.)
- Who receives notification emails
- Data retention settings

## AI provider data handling

Waterr Meet uses multiple AI providers for speech and language processing. Key guarantees:

- **No training on your data** -- Session data is not used to train any AI models
- **Ephemeral processing** -- Audio and video are processed in real-time and not stored by AI providers
- **Transcripts** -- Generated and stored on Waterr infrastructure, not by third parties
